General Info




How CRISPR is used

CRISPR is a complex system that deals with DNA. In short, proteins of T-RNA find certain segments of DNA and the CAS-9 protein cuts it out. CrRNA then replaces it with a different natural mutation or segment. Scientists can now tell T-RNA what to find and CrRNA what it should be replaced with. The CRISPR CAS-9 systems is found in any organism.

International Uses

While food is quite abundant in a lot of countries, it is scarce in certain places. GMO plants have the ability to grow in, usually resource lacking, new environments. Scientists have created rice that is both drought and flood resistant. There is also a massive amount of use in developing nations. Over 75 countries produce GMO crops with most in developing nations. Nigeria has claimed that they will not be able to feed themselves without GMO crops. As the cost to produce GMO food is becoming cheaper and cheaper, HIPC (Heavily Indebted Poor Countries) have a big opportunity get back on their feet. GMOs have the potential to put an end to poverty. There is little doubt that GMO food will be the future of humanity, if we become a space faring civilization, they will be needed. Anywhere else, they can be used for reduced land use.
